Game Jam

1 Week

Team

C#

Unity

Project Introduction

Wanted for your unforgivable crimes against the Cactus people, you, a lonesome tumbleweed - one of the last of your kind never stops tumblin'

Escape from each town, running away from the Cactus Cops as you go from town to town; hoping to escape the wild wild west.

The team

This was a team submission for the Plymouth game jam 2020 with the theme ‘Untouchable’, developed by a team of 4. This included 2 programmers, 1 designer and 1 artist and took place over 1 week in the summer of 2020.

Main Menu

One area I worked on for tumble town was the main menu, where me and the designer worked together to make diegetic UI/World space UI. The main area this is most prevalent in is the level selection where the players camera is moved over to see the level selector.

Object pooling

For a university worksheet we were tasked with optimising a previous project we had worked on, since tumble town was a game jam it was likely that it wasn’t well optimised. Although, whilst working on optimising the project I realised it wasn’t massively unoptimised due to how few features the game had. I still saw this as an opportunity to learn how to do something new.

A change that I made was to add an object pool which would hold a set number of bullets to be used by the enemies rather than having them instantiated when they are needed.

What Went well

Overall, I am happy with how tumble town turned out and how much we produced in the space of a week.

An area I am happy with is the object pooler whilst in the grand scheme of things it didn’t affect the performance that much it was something new that I learned and is something that I can use for future projects where there may be a lot more objects that get instantiated.

What Could be improved

An area I would liked to have improved on is the AI movement as the original idea was to just have stationary enemies before we added ones which would chase the player meaning they had no path navigation and just moved in the direction of the player.
